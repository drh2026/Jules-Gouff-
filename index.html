The Author

Jules Gouffé (1807–1877) was one of the most influential chefs of the nineteenth century. A student of Antonin Carême, he became Chef de Cuisine of the Paris Jockey Club and, later, chef to Napoleon III. His Le Livre de Cuisine (1867) was the era’s most systematic and practical cookery manual, bridging household and haute cuisine in a single volume.

This Volume

The English edition, translated and adapted by his brother Alphonse Gouffé — Head Pastry-Cook to Her Majesty Queen Victoria — appeared in 1869. It comprises two complete parts: Household Cookery for the domestic kitchen, and High-Class Cookery for the professional table, illustrated with sixteen colour plates and 161 woodcuts.

The Collection

This digital edition presents 1,557 recipes across 15 categories drawn from both parts of the complete 764-page work. The collection spans pot-au-feu and consommé, classical French sauces, fish and shell-fish, all the principal meats and game, pastry, sweets, cold dishes, and dessert.
